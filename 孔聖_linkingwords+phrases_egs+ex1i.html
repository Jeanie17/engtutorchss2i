📌 LINKING WORDS: ADDITION

➕ and, also, moreover, furthermore, in addition, besides

🎯 Rule: Addition linkers introduce extra information or ideas that support, reinforce, or add to a previous statement. • and → connects similar ideas (neutral).
• also / moreover / furthermore → formal/emphatic addition, often starting a new sentence.
• in addition / besides → adds extra point, often with comma.
💡 Examples: "She loves reading novels. Moreover, she writes poetry." | "The hotel was expensive; in addition, the service was poor." | "He is talented and hardworking."

⚖️ LINKING WORDS: CONTRAST & CONCESSION

but, however, although, though, even though, whereas, nevertheless, on the other hand

🎯 Rule: Contrast connectors show opposing or unexpected ideas. • but joins two contrasting clauses in one sentence.
• although / even though → introduce a subordinate clause with unexpected result.
• however / nevertheless → formal, start new sentence, followed by comma.
• whereas → highlights direct contrast between two facts.
💡 Examples: "She studied hard, but she didn’t pass." | "Although it rained, we enjoyed the picnic." | "The movie was long; however, it was fascinating."

🔍 LINKING WORDS: CAUSE & REASON

because, since, as, because of, due to, owing to, the reason why

🎯 Rule: Cause and reason connectors explain why something happens. • because / since / as → introduce subordinate clauses (since/as more formal).
• because of / due to / owing to → followed by noun/gerund.
• Position: can start or end sentence.
💡 Examples: "He passed the exam because he worked hard." | "Due to bad weather, the flight was canceled." | "Since you're here, let's start."

🎯 LINKING WORDS: PURPOSE & RESULT

so, therefore, consequently, as a result, in order to, so that, thus

🎯 Rule: Result linkers show the consequence of an action; purpose linkers indicate intention.
• so / therefore / consequently / thus → show result.
• in order to / so as to → express purpose (infinitive).
• so that → introduces purpose clause (often with modal).
💡 Examples: "It was raining, so we stayed home." | "She studied hard; therefore, she aced the test." | "He left early in order to catch the train."
