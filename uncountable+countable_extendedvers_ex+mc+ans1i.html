📘 GRAMMAR POWERPOINT: COUNTABLE vs UNCOUNTABLE NOUNS (Extended)

✅ Countable Nouns

  • Can be counted: one apple, two apples
  • Have singular & plural forms (cat/cats, child/children)
  • Use a / an with singular: a car, an umbrella
  • Use many / few / a few / several: many books, few chairs
  • Questions: How many ...? + plural noun

🚫 Uncountable Nouns

  • Cannot be counted directly: water, rice, information
  • Usually no plural form (❌ rices, advices generally wrong)
  • Use some / any / much / a little: some milk, much advice
  • Use units: a glass of water, a piece of news
  • Questions: How much ...? + uncountable noun

📌 Dual nouns: Some nouns can be both (coffee = drink uncountable, two coffees = cups countable).
🧩 Determiners: 'a/an' only with singular countable; 'some/any' with both; 'much' uncountable, 'many' countable.

📦 Countable Nouns: 30+ Common Examples

These nouns can be singular/plural, used with numbers, a/an, and "many".

✨ Tip: "a dog → dogs", "an idea → ideas", "a person → people". Countable nouns answer "How many?"

💧 Uncountable Nouns: 30+ Common Examples

These nouns are treated as singular, no plural form. Use "some/much/a little" and containers.

🧂 Remember: "information", "advice", "furniture", "luggage" → never add -s. Use "a piece of advice / an item of furniture".
