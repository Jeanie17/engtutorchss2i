📚 Key Rules: Singular & Plural Nouns

🔹 Regular plurals (add -s / -es / -ies / -ves)

• Most nouns: add -s → cat/cats, book/books.
• Nouns ending in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es → box/boxes, bus/buses, potato/potatoes.
• Nouns ending in consonant + y: change y to -ies → baby/babies, city/cities.
• Nouns ending in -f or -fe: change to -ves → leaf/leaves, knife/knives (exceptions: roof/roofs, belief/beliefs).

🔹 Irregular plurals (no -s rule)

man/men, woman/women, child/children, tooth/teeth, foot/feet, mouse/mice, goose/geese, person/people, ox/oxen.

🔹 Nouns always plural (plural in form, often take plural verb)

scissors, trousers, glasses (eyewear), jeans, belongings, congratulations, thanks, outskirts, goods, premises.
Example: "These scissors are sharp."

🔹 Nouns that are singular but look plural

news, mathematics, physics, economics, measles, mumps, billiards, darts.
"The news is good." / "Mathematics is my favorite subject."

🔹 Same singular and plural form

sheep, deer, fish (fishes for species), aircraft, species, series.
