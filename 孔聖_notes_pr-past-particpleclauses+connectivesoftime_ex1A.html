📖 PRESENT PARTICIPLE CLAUSES

active meaning · simultaneous or sequential actions · -ing form

🎯 GRAMMATIC RULE: Present participle clauses (using -ing) give active meaning. They describe actions happening at the same time as the main verb, or give reasons/causes. They make writing more concise.
• Form: (while) + present participle (verb+ing) — often without a subject.
• Use: to express two actions by the same subject; to replace a relative clause with active meaning; to show cause/reason.
• Example: Walking home, I saw a shooting star. (while I was walking)
• Feeling tired, she went to bed. (because she felt tired)
💡 Model examples: “Running to catch the bus, he dropped his phone.” | “Not knowing the answer, she remained silent.” | “The man sitting next to me was asleep.”

📘 PAST PARTICIPLE CLAUSES

passive meaning · having been + past participle · reduced passive clauses

🎯 GRAMMATIC RULE: Past participle clauses (usually -ed, -en, -t) have a passive meaning. They often replace a passive relative clause or show reason/circumstance in a formal style.
• Form: past participle + (by agent) — the subject receives the action.
• Use: to give reasons, conditions, or to add information concisely.
• Example: Exhausted by the journey, they rested. (because they were exhausted)
• The books written by the author are famous. (which were written)
💡 Model examples: “Shocked by the news, she couldn't speak.” | “Given more time, he would have succeeded.” | “The window broken yesterday has been fixed.”

⏱️ CONNECTIVES OF TIME

when, while, as, after, before, until, as soon as, once, by the time, whenever

🎯 GRAMMATIC RULE: Time connectives (subordinating conjunctions) link a time clause to a main clause to show the sequence or duration of events. They answer “when?”.
• Common connectives: when, while, as, after, before, until/till, as soon as, once, by the time, whenever.
• Important: In future time clauses, we use present simple (not will) after these connectives.
• Example: I'll call you as soon as I arrive. (NOT will arrive)
• While she was cooking, the phone rang.
💡 Model examples: “After finishing his homework, he went out.” | “Stay here until I come back.” | “Whenever I see her, she smiles.”
