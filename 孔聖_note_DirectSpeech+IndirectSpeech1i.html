💬 DIRECT SPEECH

Quoting exact words · Quotation marks · Reporting clause · Punctuation rules

🎯 GRAMMATIC RULES: Direct speech repeats the exact words spoken, enclosed in quotation marks (inverted commas). It is often accompanied by a reporting clause (e.g., he said, she asked).

• Quotation marks: Use double (“ ”) or single (‘ ’) quotes consistently.
• Punctuation inside quotes: Commas, full stops, question marks, and exclamation marks go inside the closing quotation mark.
• Reporting clause position: Can come before, after, or in the middle of the speech.
• Capitalization: The first word of the direct speech is capitalized.
• New speaker = new paragraph: Start a new line when a different person speaks.

✅ Examples: She said, “I'm going to the market.”
“What time is it?” he asked.
“I'm tired,” she whispered, “and I want to go home.”
💡 Model examples: “I love this song,” she exclaimed. | He shouted, “Watch out!” | “Is anyone home?” called Sarah.

📢 REPORTED SPEECH

Indirect speech · Tense backshift · Pronoun changes · Time/place changes · Reporting verbs

🎯 GRAMMATIC RULES: Reported speech (indirect speech) reports what someone said without quoting their exact words. Tense, pronouns, and time/place references often change.

• Tense backshift (when reporting verb is past):
  Present Simple → Past Simple
  Present Continuous → Past Continuous
  Present Perfect → Past Perfect
  Past Simple → Past Perfect
  will → would, can → could, may → might
• Pronoun changes: Adjust pronouns to match the perspective of the reporter.
• Time/place changes: now → then, today → that day, here → there, yesterday → the day before, tomorrow → the next day, this → that
• Questions: Use if/whether for yes/no questions; keep wh- word for wh- questions. Use statement word order (subject + verb).
• Commands/requests: Use tell/ask + object + to-infinitive.
• Reporting verbs: said (that), told me (that), asked, explained, suggested, etc.

✅ Example: Direct: “I am tired,” she said. → Reported: She said (that) she was tired.
💡 Model examples: “I'll call you tomorrow” → He said he would call me the next day.
“Where do you live?” → She asked where I lived.
“Please sit down” → He asked me to sit down.
